Empowering Indian Citizens with AI Legal Aid

Accessing legal justice in India can be daunting due to expensive lawyer fees and complex procedures. Ask Lawyer is here to change that. We utilize cutting-edge Artificial Intelligence to demystify Indian laws, making them accessible to every citizen.

Whether you are dealing with a property dispute, a consumer issue, or police harassment, our AI tool provides instant, preliminary guidance based on the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS), Indian Penal Code (IPC), and other relevant statutes.

Our Comprehensive Legal Services

1. Automated Legal Drafting

Legal documentation is the foundation of any case. Our tool helps you generate drafts for:

  • Legal Notices: For cheque bounce (Section 138 NI Act), recovery of dues, or breach of contract.
  • Rental Agreements: Standard 11-month lease agreements compliant with local Rent Control Acts.
  • Resignation & Employment Letters: Professional templates for corporate needs.

2. Consumer Complaint Assistance

Defective product? Unfair trade practice? The Consumer Protection Act, 2019 empowers you. We guide you on:

  • Sending a mandatory legal notice to the seller/company.
  • Filing a complaint online via E-Daakhil.
  • Understanding the jurisdiction (District vs State Commission).

3. Criminal Law Guidance (FIR & NCR)

Understanding police procedures is crucial for safety. We explain:

  • Zero FIR: Your right to file an FIR at any police station regardless of jurisdiction.
  • BNS vs IPC: How the new criminal laws affect your case.
  • Bail Procedures: Information on bailable vs non-bailable offenses.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Is the legal advice here 100% accurate?

Our AI strives for high accuracy based on current laws. However, AI can make errors. Always verify critical information with a qualified advocate.

Do you charge for legal drafts?

No, all current services on Ask Lawyer are completely free of cost as part of our social initiative.

Can I use these drafts in court?

The drafts provided are templates. You should get them reviewed, printed on stamp paper, and notarized as per legal requirements before submission.
