How Ask Lawyer Works

We believe getting initial legal guidance shouldn't be complicated or expensive. Here is how you can use our AI assistant to get the clarity you need:

  • Describe Your Issue: Simply type your legal problem in the chat box above. You can write in everyday English or Roman Hindi (e.g., "Mera rent agreement khatam ho gaya hai...").
  • AI Analysis: Our system quickly scans updated Indian laws, including the new Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS) and the Consumer Protection Act, to find relevant sections for your case.
  • Actionable Guidance: You receive a clear, jargon-free explanation of your rights, along with practical next steps or a template for legal drafting.

Comprehensive Legal Guidance for Indian Citizens

Navigating the legal landscape in India can often seem daunting. With thousands of statutes, changing amendments like the transition from the Indian Penal Code (IPC) to the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS), and complex judicial precedents, having access to clear and accurate legal information is essential. Ask Lawyer is designed to bridge the gap between complex legal jargon and everyday understanding.

Automated Legal Drafting & Documentation

Legal documentation is the foundation of any strong case or formal agreement. A poorly drafted document can lead to severe legal complications down the line. Our AI tool assists you in generating foundational drafts for various scenarios. Whether it is a notice for cheque bounce under Section 138 of the Negotiable Instruments Act, recovery of pending dues, or a breach of a formal contract, our tool helps you structure your demands clearly. You can also draft standard 11-month lease agreements that are compliant with local Rent Control Acts, protecting both landlords and tenants.

Navigating the Consumer Protection Act

Have you been sold a defective product or faced an unfair trade practice? The Consumer Protection Act, 2019 strongly empowers buyers. However, many are unaware of the procedural steps. We guide you on how to send a mandatory legal notice to the defaulting seller, how to utilize the E-Daakhil portal for online complaint filing, and how to determine the correct jurisdiction (District, State, or National Commission) based on the value of your claim.

About Ask Lawyer Initiative

Ask Lawyer is a socio-legal initiative thoughtfully developed by Gahininath Gutte. Recognizing the significant gap in legal literacy across rural and urban India, this platform was envisioned to serve as a "First Line of Defense" for the common citizen.

Many individuals hesitate to seek legal help due to the fear of exorbitant consultation fees or the intimidation of complex court procedures. Our core mission is summarized simply: "Legal Literacy for All." By removing the barriers of complex legal language and prohibitive costs, we aim to empower individuals to understand their fundamental rights and take informed legal steps.
